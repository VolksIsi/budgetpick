How to Make a Cheap Microphone Sound Professional

You've purchased an affordable mic like the SUDOTACK ST-800, but out of the box, it lacks the 'radio' punch you hear on popular podcasts. Don't worry—90% of a microphone's sound comes down to placement and processing.

1. Microphone Placement is Everything

Get the mic within 4 to 6 inches of your mouth. The closer you are to the microphone, the richer your voice will sound (a phenomenon known as the proximity effect) and the lower you can turn down the microphone's gain, drastically reducing background room noise or keyboard clicks.

2. Use Free VST Plugins (EQ and Compression)

Download a free software like OBS Studio or Voicemeeter. Add an EQ (Equalizer) filter to roll off the muddy low-end frequencies and slightly boost the high frequencies for clarity. Next, add a Compressor. A compressor automatically evens out your volume—making your quiet whispers audible while preventing your louder shouts from distorting.

3. Treat Your Room, Not Just Your Mic

Even a $3,000 microphone sounds terrible in an empty, echoing room. Put down rugs, close your curtains, and add soft furniture to your space to deaden acoustic reflections.
