USB vs XLR: Why USB is King for Budget Setups

The audio world is packed with terminology that can confuse beginners. The most common question we get at BudgetPick is: Should I buy a USB or XLR microphone?

The Short Answer

If your entire audio budget is under $50, you should absolutely buy a USB microphone like the Fifine K669B USB or the Samson Go Mic Portable.

The Long Answer

XLR microphones represent the professional standard. They transmit a clean analog signal but require an external Audio Interface to convert that analog signal into a digital one your PC can understand via USB. A decent entry-level audio interface alone costs $100+.

USB microphones have the audio interface (the Analog-to-Digital converter) built directly into the microphone housing. This means you only need one single wire. You plug it in, your computer recognizes it instantly, and you start recording.
