Condenser vs Dynamic Microphones

When shopping for budget mics, you'll constantly encounter two words: Condenser and Dynamic. Understanding the difference will save you from making a huge purchasing mistake.

Condenser Microphones

Almost all USB microphones under $50 (like the Behringer C-1U) are Condensers. Condenser mics are extremely sensitive and capture highly detailed, bright, and nuanced audio. They are perfect for quiet, carpeted, sound-treated rooms. Warning: Because they are so sensitive, they will pick up your mechanical keyboard, your PC fan, and the dog barking next door.

Dynamic Microphones

Dynamic microphones operate on electromagnetism. They are much less sensitive but are renowned for their incredible background noise rejection. If you stream in a noisy house, a dynamic mic is a lifesaver. However, they are rare to find with USB connections under $50, and usually require expensive audio interfaces to power them properly.

Verdict: On a strict budget under $50, stick to Condenser USB mics, but invest time in learning how to use software noise gates!
